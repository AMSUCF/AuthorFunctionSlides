I close by addressing one area where responses to large AI models have been surprisingly reactionary—that of intellectual property. Objections to large AI models on copyright grounds amount to a dramatic reversal of attitudes among the American left, what Kirschenbaum and Raley call a "new copyright fundamentalism." Two decades ago, many people now urging copyright infringement claims against AI firms were deriding Metallica and the RIAA for their lawsuits against Napster and its successors. Back then, piracy was cool; we called it "sharing."
